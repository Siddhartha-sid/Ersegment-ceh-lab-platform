🎭 Module 9: Social Engineering

Manipulating human psychology to gain unauthorized access or information.

Lab Progress: 0%

🧠 Human-Based Social Engineering

  • Pretexting: Creating a fabricated scenario to obtain information.
  • Baiting: Offering something enticing (e.g., free USB drive) to deliver malware.
  • Quid Pro Quo: Promising a service in exchange for information.
  • Tailgating: Following authorized personnel into restricted areas.
  • Vishing: Voice phishing over phone calls.
  • Impersonation: Pretending to be a trusted authority (IT support, CEO).

💻 Computer-Based Social Engineering

  • Phishing: Mass emails with malicious links/attachments.
  • Spear Phishing: Targeted phishing at specific individuals.
  • Whaling: Phishing aimed at executives.
  • Clone Phishing: Resending a legitimate email with malicious replacement.
  • Watering Hole: Compromising websites frequented by targets.

🛡️ Countermeasures

MeasureDescription
Security Awareness TrainingRegularly educate employees about social engineering tactics.
Phishing SimulationsTest and reinforce awareness with mock campaigns.
Multi-Factor AuthenticationMitigates credential theft.
Strict Access ControlsLimits damage from successful attacks.
